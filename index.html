About

I build quantitative models to study tumor evolution and therapeutic response. My work combines branching processes, ODEs, and SDEs with probabilistic and statistical inference to uncover intratumoral heterogeneity, evaluate therapeutic mechanisms, and guide therapy design and optimization.

Advisor: Prof. Kevin Leder ‚Ä¢ B.Sc. (Applied Math), Fudan University

Research

Mathematical modeling of cancer dynamics; stochastic processes; tumor‚Äìimmune interactions; parameter estimation; therapy design.

  • Stochastic & deterministic models (branching processes, ODE/SDE) of tumor progression and therapy response.
  • Inference and identifiability: parameter estimation, profile likelihoods, uncertainty quantification.
  • Applications to immunotherapy (CISH-KO T cells, checkpoint blockade) and radiotherapy (GBM, optimal dosing).

Research in Progress

Mathematical Modeling of MDSC-Mediated Immunosuppression in Glioblastoma under Radiotherapy

ODE model of the GBM tumor‚Äìimmune microenvironment quantifying MDSC-driven suppression under radiotherapy; calibrated to clinical data to explore optimal RT regimes and run in-silico virtual trials. Collaborators: Kevin Leder, Jasmine Foo, Lindsey Sloan, John Metzcar.

Mathematical Modeling of CISH-Knockout T Cells with Checkpoint Inhibition

Population dynamical model of endogenous and infused CISH-KO T cells, IL-2 dynamics, and tumor burden; analyzes competition for IL-2 and synergy with checkpoint blockade to predict conditions for durable response. Collaborators: Kevin Leder, Jasmine Foo, Emil Lou, Kamran Kaveh, Sarah Anderson.
